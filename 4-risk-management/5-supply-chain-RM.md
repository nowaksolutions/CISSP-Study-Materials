<h1 align="center">🔗 Supply Chain Risk Management (SCRM)</h1>
<p align="center"><strong>Third-Party Risk • Vendor Trust • Procurement Security • Operational Resilience</strong></p>

---

## 📘 Concept

**Supply Chain Risk Management (SCRM)** is the process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks introduced by:

- Vendors  
- Suppliers  
- Service providers  
- Product lifecycles  

<p align="center"><strong>Third-Party Risk = Organizational Risk</strong></p>

External dependencies must not introduce **vulnerabilities, disruptions, or compromise**.

---

## 📚 Key References

**NIST IR 7622**  
> Guidance on identifying and mitigating supply chain risks across the lifecycle  

**CNSSD 505**  
> Defines minimum security requirements for national security systems  

**ISO/IEC 28000**  
> Global standard for **supply chain security management systems**

---

## 🧩 Core SCRM Functions

### 🎯 Purpose
> Identify risks across the supply chain

- Evaluate **vendors, suppliers, and dependencies**  
- Identify potential **security and operational impacts**

---

### 🔍 Risk Analysis
> Assess vendor trust and risk exposure

- Security posture  
- Operational reliability  
- Access to sensitive data or systems  

---

### ⚖️ Compliance
> Enforce legal and contractual obligations

- Regulatory requirements  
- Security expectations  
- Contractual accountability  

---

## ⚠️ Types of Supply Chain Risk

**Unintentional Risks**  
- Misconfigurations  
- Weak security practices  
- Lack of awareness  

---

**Service Risks**  
- Cloud providers  
- Managed services  
- Outsourced operations  

---

**Hardware Risks**  
- Counterfeit components  
- Tampered devices  
- Embedded backdoors  

---

**Software Risks**  
- Vulnerable code  
- Malicious libraries  
- Insecure updates  

---

## 🛡️ Security Requirements

- Define **minimum baseline controls** for all vendors  
- Establish **secure procurement strategies**  
- Enforce **SLAs with security accountability**  

<p align="center"><strong>Contracts must enforce security, not just define service</strong></p>

---

## 🔎 Supply Chain Assessment Methods

### 🏢 On-Site Assessments
> Direct evaluation of vendor operations

- Highest level of assurance  
- Verifies **actual implementation**  
- Resource-intensive  

---

### 📄 Documentation Review
> Evaluate vendor-provided materials

- Policies, certifications, reports  
- Scalable and cost-effective  
- Relies on **self-reported data**  

---

### 🧾 Third-Party Audits
> Independent external validation

- Objective and standardized  
- May not address **organization-specific risks**  

---

## ⚠️ Critical Principle

**Trust must be verified, not assumed.**

<p align="center"><strong>Assess → Enforce → Monitor</strong></p>

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

Falls under **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)** and represents:

- Third-party risk  
- Operational risk  
- Supply chain compromise risk  

Failure leads to:

- Breaches through vendors  
- Data exposure  
- Operational disruption  

CISSP questions will test your ability to:

- Identify **vendor-related risks**  
- Enforce **contractual controls (SLA/security clauses)**  
- Select appropriate **assessment methods**

---

## 🧠 CISSP Decision Lens

When evaluating a scenario:

1. Does a **third party have access or dependency**?  
2. Has the vendor been **assessed and validated**?  
3. Are **security requirements enforced contractually**?  
4. Is there **ongoing monitoring of vendor risk**?  

Default mindset:

**Third parties extend your attack surface**

---

## 🚨 Exam Trap

- Assuming vendors are secure without validation  
- Relying only on **contracts without verification**  
- Skipping **ongoing monitoring after onboarding**

---

## ✅ Exam Takeaway

**Supply chain risk is your risk.**

- Assess vendors before engagement  
- Enforce security through contracts  
- Continuously monitor third-party risk
