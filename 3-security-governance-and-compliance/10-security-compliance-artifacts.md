<h1 align="center">📜 Security Compliance Artifacts</h1>
<p align="center"><strong>Policy Hierarchy • Governance • Control Translation</strong></p>

---

## 📘 Concept

Security compliance artifacts are **structured governance documents** that translate:

- **External requirements** (laws, regulations, contracts)  
→ into  
- **Internal controls and operational practices**

They define **what must be done, how it is enforced, and how it is executed**.

---

## 🧩 Policy Hierarchy (Top-Down Model)

<p align="center"><strong>Law / Regulation → Policy → Standard → Baseline → Procedure / Guideline</strong></p>

---

### 🏛️ Policy (Intent & Authority)
> High-level, mandatory directive

- Defines **organizational intent, scope, and responsibilities**  
- Establishes **compliance requirements**  
- **Broad, stable, rarely changes**

#### Types of Policies:
- **Organizational Policy** – Enterprise-wide governance and structure  
- **Issue-Specific Policy** – Focused areas (AUP, email, remote access)  
- **System-Specific Policy** – Applies to specific systems or technologies  

---

### 📏 Standard (Mandatory Requirements)
> Defines **what must be done**

- Mandatory and enforceable  
- **Specific and measurable**  
- Ensures **consistency across the organization**

---

### 📊 Baseline (Minimum Acceptable State)
> Defines the **minimum required configuration**

- Derived from standards  
- Represents **floor, not ceiling**  
- Often evolves into enforced requirements  

---

### ⚙️ Procedure (Execution)
> Defines **how to perform tasks**

- Step-by-step instructions  
- Operational and actionable  
- Used by administrators and operators  

---

### 🧭 Guideline (Advisory)
> Provides **recommended best practices**

- Not mandatory  
- Flexible implementation  
- Supports standards and baselines  

---

## ⚠️ Critical Principle

Each document serves a **distinct role in governance**:

<p align="center"><strong>Policy = Intent  
Standard = Requirement  
Baseline = Minimum  
Procedure = Execution  
Guideline = Recommendation</strong></p>

---

## 🔄 Governance Lifecycle Requirements

- Policies remain **stable**, supporting long-term governance  
- Standards, baselines, and procedures are **updated frequently**  
- Documents must:
  - Follow **consistent templates**  
  - Be sourced from **authoritative frameworks**  
  - Undergo **regular review (due diligence)**  
  - Include **version control and approval processes**  

---

## ⚖️ Exceptions and Risk Acceptance

Organizations must define formal processes for:

- **Exceptions and waivers**  
- **Risk acceptance decisions**  
- **Documentation and approval**  

No control deviation should occur without **documented justification**.

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

Falls under **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)** and represents:

- Governance risk  
- Compliance risk  
- Audit and enforcement risk  

Poor artifact management leads to:

- Inconsistent control implementation  
- Compliance failures  
- Audit findings  
- Increased organizational risk  

CISSP questions will test your ability to:

- Identify the **correct document type**  
- Determine what needs to be **created or updated**  
- Enforce compliance through **proper governance structure**

---

## 🧠 CISSP Decision Lens

When evaluating a scenario:

- Is this defining **intent or direction**? → Policy  
- Is this defining **mandatory requirements**? → Standard  
- Is this defining a **minimum configuration**? → Baseline  
- Is this explaining **how to perform a task**? → Procedure  
- Is this offering **flexible guidance**? → Guideline  

Default mindset:

**Intent → Requirement → Minimum → Execution**

---

## 🚨 Exam Trap

- Confusing **standards vs baselines vs procedures**  
- Treating **guidelines as mandatory**  
- Skipping policy and jumping directly to implementation  

---

## ✅ Exam Takeaway

**Policy drives intent  
Standard enforces requirements  
Baseline sets minimums  
Procedure executes  
Guideline advises**
