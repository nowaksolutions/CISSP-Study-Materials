## 📘 Concept

**Cybercrime** is any criminal act involving a computing device as the **target or tool**.

A **Data Breach** is a specific incident where **sensitive or protected information is accessed, disclosed, or stolen without authorization**.

Common causes:

- External attacks  
- Insider threats  
- Misconfigurations  
- Weak or missing controls  

Typical data exposed:

- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**  
- Financial data  
- Intellectual property  

---

## ⚖️ Core U.S. Cyber Laws

### 🛑 CFAA (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act)
> Primary U.S. anti-hacking law

- Criminalizes **unauthorized access** or **exceeding authorized access**  
- Covers:
  - Hacking  
  - Malware activity  
  - Fraud involving protected systems  
- Applies to:
  - Government systems  
  - Financial systems  
  - Systems used in **interstate or foreign commerce**  
- Includes **criminal penalties + civil lawsuits**

---

### ⚡ NIIPA (National Information Infrastructure Protection Act)
> Expansion of CFAA

- Extends protection to **critical infrastructure systems**  
- Broadens definition of **protected computers**  
- Increases penalties for attacks on:
  - Power grids  
  - Telecommunications  
  - Transportation  

---

### 🏛️ FISMA (Federal Information Security Modernization Act)
> Federal security governance law

Applies to **U.S. federal agencies and contractors**

Requires:

- **Continuous monitoring**  
- **Risk assessments and system categorization**  
- **Implementation of NIST-based controls**  
- **Annual reporting and accountability**

👉 Focus: **ongoing risk management, not one-time compliance**

---

## ⚠️ Legal Reality

- Laws apply based on:
  - **Jurisdiction**
  - **System location**
  - **Data location**
- Violations can result in:
  - Criminal charges  
  - Civil liability  
  - Regulatory enforcement  

---

## 🧑‍💻 Threat Actors

- **Attackers** – disrupt, destroy, or gain access  
- **Hackers** – execute technical attacks  
- **Script Kiddies** – low-skill, tool-based attackers  
- **Insider Threats** – abuse authorized access  
- **State Actors** – advanced, government-backed operations  

---

## 🎯 Types of Attacks

**Active Attacks**  
- Modify or disrupt systems/data  

**Passive Attacks**  
- Observe and gather information without detection  

**Zero-Day Attacks**  
- Exploit **unknown or unpatched vulnerabilities**

---

## 💥 Disruption-Based Crimes

- **DoS / DDoS** – overwhelm systems using bots/zombies  
- **DNS Poisoning** – redirect users via corrupted DNS data  

---

## 🔥 Destruction & High-Impact Crimes

- Intentional destruction of systems or data  
- **Cyberterrorism** – large-scale disruption and fear  
- **Improper Sanitization** – failure to securely destroy data  

---

## 🎭 Hacktivism

Motivated by **political, ideological, or social causes**

Common activities:

- Website defacement  
- **Doxxing** (exposing private information)

---

## ⚠️ Critical Principle

**Unauthorized access is the foundation of most cybercrime laws.**

<p align="center"><strong>Unauthorized Access → Legal Violation → Enforcement</strong></p>

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

Spans:

- **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)**  
- **Security Operations (Domain 7)**  

Represents:

- Legal risk  
- Operational risk  
- Incident response and enforcement risk  

CISSP questions will test your ability to:

- Identify **which law applies**  
- Recognize **unauthorized access scenarios**  
- Align actions with **legal and organizational response**

---

## 🧠 CISSP Decision Lens

When evaluating a scenario:

1. Is there **unauthorized or excessive access**?  
2. Does it involve **critical infrastructure**?  
3. Is it within a **federal system or contractor scope**?  
4. What response aligns with **legal obligations**?  

Default mindset:

**Identify the violation → Map to law → Respond appropriately**

---

## 🚨 Exam Trap

- Misunderstanding **“exceeding authorized access”** under CFAA  
- Confusing **governance (FISMA)** with criminal law  
- Ignoring **jurisdictional scope**

---

## ✅ Exam Takeaway

- **CFAA** → Unauthorized access  
- **NIIPA** → Critical infrastructure expansion  
- **FISMA** → Federal security governance and continuous monitoring
