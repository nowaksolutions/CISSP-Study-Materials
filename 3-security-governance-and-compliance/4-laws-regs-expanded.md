<p align="center"><strong>Data Protection • Privacy • Financial Integrity • Compliance</strong></p>

---

## 📘 Overview

These laws establish **legal obligations** for protecting data, privacy, and financial systems.

Your objective is simple:

<p align="center"><strong>Identify the Data → Match the Law → Apply the Requirement</strong></p>

---

## 🧩 Regulatory Landscape

### 🛡️ Consumer Protection

**FTC Act (Federal Trade Commission Act)**  
> Prohibits **unfair or deceptive practices**

- Enforces **reasonable data protection expectations**
- Applies broadly across industries
- Used when organizations fail to **protect consumer data**

---

### 💳 Financial Data Protection

**GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act)**  
> Protects **customer financial information**

- Applies to **financial institutions**
- Requires **safeguards + privacy notices**
- Focus: **confidentiality of financial data**

---

### 📡 Communications Privacy

**ECPA (Electronic Communications Privacy Act)**  
> Protects **electronic communications**

- Covers **email, voice, and data in transit**
- Prevents **unauthorized interception/access**

---

### 🏥 Health Data Protection

**HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)**  
> Protects **Protected Health Information (PHI)**

- Requires **administrative, technical, physical safeguards**

**HITECH Act**  
> Strengthens HIPAA enforcement

- Adds **breach notification requirements**
- Expands scope to **business associates**
- Increases **penalties and enforcement**

---

### 🧬 Sensitive Personal Data

**GINA (Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act)**  
> Protects **genetic information**

- Prevents discrimination in:
  - Health insurance  
  - Employment  

---

### 📊 Corporate Governance & Reporting

**SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)**  
> Ensures **accurate financial reporting**

- Applies to **public companies**
- Requires:
  - **Internal controls**
  - **Audit integrity**
  - **IT controls supporting financial systems**

---

## ⚠️ Critical Distinction

<p align="center"><strong>LAW = Legal Requirement  
  
PCI DSS = Contractual Obligation</strong></p>

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

- Domain: **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)**  
- Focus: **Regulatory and compliance risk**

Failure results in:

- Legal penalties  
- Regulatory enforcement  
- Business disruption  
- Loss of trust  

CISSP questions will test:
- **Law identification by data type**
- **Regulatory vs contractual understanding**

---

## 🚨 Exam Trap

- Treating **PCI DSS as a law**
- Misidentifying the **data type being protected**
- Overthinking instead of mapping directly

---

## ✅ Exam Takeaway

**Match the law to the data. Every time.**

- Financial → GLBA  
- Health → HIPAA/HITECH  
- Corporate → SOX  
- Consumer → FTC  
- Communications → ECPA  
- Genetic → GINA  
- Payment Cards → PCI DSS (contractual)
