## 🔐 Due Care vs Due Diligence

---

### 📘 Concept

**Due Care** and **Due Diligence** are **legal and governance principles**, not technical controls.

They define:
- **What protections must be implemented**
- **How those protections are validated over time**

---

### 🧩 Core Distinction

**Due Care**  
Represents the **actions taken to protect assets, people, and data**.  
Answers: *“Are we doing what a reasonable organization should do to prevent harm?”*

Includes:
- Policies and standards  
- Access controls  
- Security safeguards  
- Monitoring controls  

👉 Think: **Do the right thing (implement protection)**

---

**Due Diligence**  
Represents the **ongoing effort to verify and maintain those protections**.  
Answers: *“Are we continuously ensuring our protections are effective?”*

Includes:
- Risk assessments  
- Audits and reviews  
- Continuous monitoring  
- Training and improvement  

👉 Think: **Prove and maintain protection over time**

### ⚠️ Lifecycle Relationship

These are not separate. They operate in a continuous cycle:

1. Requirement identified  
2. **Due Diligence** → Research and analysis  
3. **Due Care** → Implement safeguards  
4. **Due Diligence** → Monitor, audit, and improve  

<p align="center"><strong>Implement → Validate → Improve (Continuous Cycle)</strong></p>

---

### 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

Falls under **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)** and represents **legal, regulatory, and liability risk**.

Failure results in:

- Negligence claims  
- Regulatory penalties  
- Loss of stakeholder trust  
- Increased organizational risk exposure  

CISSP questions will test your ability to distinguish between:
- **Implementing controls (Due Care)**  
- **Assessing and maintaining them (Due Diligence)**  

---

### 🧠 CISSP Decision Lens

When evaluating a scenario:

- Is the action **building or implementing a control**? → Due Care  
- Is the action **reviewing, auditing, or improving**? → Due Diligence  

Default mindset:

- **Due Care = Protect**  
- **Due Diligence = Verify and maintain**

---

### 🚨 Exam Trap

Confusing **implementation (Due Care)** with **ongoing validation and monitoring (Due Diligence)**.

---

### ✅ Exam Takeaway

**Due Care = Implement safeguards  
Due Diligence = Continuously verify and maintain them**

Both are required to **avoid negligence**.
