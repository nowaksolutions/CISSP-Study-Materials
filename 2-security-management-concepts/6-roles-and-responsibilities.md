## 🔐 Roles and Responsibilities in Security Governance

---

### 📘 Concept

Effective security governance requires **clear separation of duties** to ensure **accountability, proper oversight, and reduced risk**.

Roles are divided between **leadership, operational execution, data ownership, and system support**, each with distinct responsibilities.

---

### 🧩 Executive & Governance Roles

**Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**  
Provides **overall organizational leadership and accountability**.

**Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**  
Oversees **financial risk, budgeting, and cost-related decision-making**.

**Chief Information Officer (CIO)**  
Leads **IT strategy, infrastructure, and technology alignment with business goals**.

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**  
Owns the **information security program and enterprise risk management strategy**.

**Board / Committees**  
Provide **governance, oversight, and strategic direction**.

---

### ⚙️ Operational Security Roles

**Information Systems Security Manager (ISSM)**  
Develops and manages the **security program and policies**.

**Information System Security Officer (ISSO)**  
Implements and maintains **security controls and compliance activities**.

**Security Analyst**  
Monitors, detects, and analyzes **threats and security events**.

**Security Engineer**  
Designs and implements **security solutions and architectures**.

---

### 🗂️ Data Roles (Critical for Exam)

**Data Owner**  
Has **ultimate accountability** for data classification and protection.  
Defines **access requirements and security controls**.

**Data Custodian**  
Implements and maintains **controls defined by the owner**.

**Data Steward**  
Ensures **data quality, consistency, and proper usage**.

---

### 🖥️ System & Support Roles

**System Administrator**  
Manages **systems, configurations, and infrastructure operations**.

**Security Auditor**  
Evaluates **control effectiveness, compliance, and risk posture**.

**User**  
Must follow **security policies and protect credentials**.

---

### ⚠️ Critical Principle

**Accountability and implementation must be separated.**

- **Owners = accountable**  
- **Custodians / Admins = implement controls**

<p align="center"><strong>Ownership ≠ Implementation</strong></p>

---

### 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

Spans **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)** and **Identity and Access Management (Domain 5)**.

Poor role definition leads to:

- Gaps in control ownership  
- Increased insider threat risk  
- Misuse of access or authority  
- Breakdown in accountability  

CISSP questions will test your ability to **identify who is responsible vs who executes**.

---

### 🧠 CISSP Decision Lens

When evaluating a scenario:

1. Identify who has **accountability (decision authority)**  
2. Identify who performs **implementation (execution)**  
3. Ensure duties are **properly separated**

Always default to:

- **Owner = decision-maker**  
- **Custodian/Admin = executor**

---

### 🚨 Exam Trap

Assigning responsibility for **data protection or classification** to custodians or administrators instead of the **data owner**.

---

### ✅ Exam Takeaway

**Ownership = accountability.  
Custodian = implementation.**
