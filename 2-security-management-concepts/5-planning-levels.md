## 🔐 Planning Levels in Security Management

---

### 📘 Concept

Security planning operates across **three distinct levels**, each with a defined scope and time horizon.

These levels must remain **aligned**, with each layer supporting execution of the one above it.

---

### 🧩 Planning Levels (Hierarchy)

**Strategic Plan (3–5 years)**  
Defines the **long-term vision**, aligned with **business objectives and risk posture**.  
Focuses on **direction, priorities, and high-level goals**.

**Tactical Plan (9–12 months)**  
Translates strategy into **specific initiatives, projects, and programs**.  
Focuses on **how strategic objectives will be achieved**.

**Operational Plan (Short-term / Daily)**  
Executes through **day-to-day activities, procedures, and tasks**.  
Focuses on **implementation and ongoing operations**.

---

### ⚠️ Critical Principle

Each level must **directly support and align with the level above it**.

Misalignment results in **ineffective execution and wasted resources**.

<p align="center"><strong>Strategic → Tactical → Operational</strong></p>

---

### 🎯 Why This Matters (CISSP Context)

Falls under **Security and Risk Management (Domain 1)** and represents **planning and execution risk**.

Poor alignment leads to:

- Disconnected security initiatives  
- Inefficient use of resources  
- Failure to meet business objectives  
- Increased organizational risk  

CISSP questions will test your ability to **identify the correct planning level based on scope and timeframe**.

---

### 🧠 CISSP Decision Lens

Determine the correct level by asking:

- Is this defining **long-term direction**? → Strategic  
- Is this executing a **project or initiative**? → Tactical  
- Is this a **daily task or procedure**? → Operational  

Always match the **scope and timeframe** to the correct level.

---

### 🚨 Exam Trap

Confusing **tactical actions (projects)** with **strategic decisions (direction and vision)**.

---

### ✅ Exam Takeaway

**Strategic sets direction → Tactical executes initiatives → Operational performs daily work.**
